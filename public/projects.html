Design and Analysis of Marine Propeller Blade

Overview

In this project, I selected three materials—CFRP, GFRP, and Aluminum—for the design and analysis of a marine propeller blade. I designed the propeller blade using CAD software and performed structural analysis with ANSYS. The goal was to evaluate the strength and corrosion resistance of each material.

Project Details

  • Material Selection: Selected CFRP, GFRP, and Aluminum based on their tensile strength, weight, and corrosion resistance.
  • Design: Designed the propeller blade using CAD software.
  • Analysis: Performed structural analysis using ANSYS, focusing on tensile strength, stress distribution, and corrosion resistance.
  • Findings: CFRP and GFRP exhibited higher tensile strength and reduced corrosion compared to Aluminum.

Background

This project evaluates materials and performs structural analysis for the design of a marine propeller blade. Materials like CFRP, GFRP, and Aluminum were selected for their strength and corrosion resistance.

Objectives

  • Analyze material strength and durability.
  • Perform structural simulations using ANSYS.
  • Enhance understanding of material performance in marine applications.

Technologies Used

  • CAD Software (e.g., SolidWorks)
  • ANSYS (for structural analysis)
  • Material Selection Techniques
